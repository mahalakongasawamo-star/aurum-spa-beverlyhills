# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Executives in transit through LAX who want a high-end medical-aesthetics retreat compressed into a single day or half-day before continuing their travel. Time-poor, financially unconstrained, privacy-sensitive. They are not researching providers across many tabs — they are deciding whether Aurum is the kind of place that receives someone like them. The decisive action is booking the **Aurum Full Day Retreat** ($1,350 / 6 hr), with the Half-Day journeys ($625 / $825) as fallbacks.

## Product Purpose

Aurum Spa & Aesthetics is a medical-aesthetics destination on Wilshire Boulevard, Beverly Hills. The site exists to convert a narrow but high-value visitor — the layover-class executive — into a booked appointment for a signature journey. Success is not a high traffic count; success is the right person feeling, within thirty seconds, that this place is for them. Everything on the page should read as a private invitation, not a public solicitation.

## Brand Personality

**Quiet-luxury. Authoritative. Restorative.**

Three emotional layers must coexist in every surface:
- **Exclusivity** — the sense of being let in to something not everyone gets.
- **Medical rigor** — confidence that the expertise is real, the protocols are precise, the supervision is medical.
- **Opulence** — the unapologetic message that the visitor deserves this.

Voice is hushed and self-assured. Never excited, never urgent, never apologetic. Closer to a concierge speaking softly than to a brand speaking loudly.

## Anti-references

The site must NOT look like:
- **Lavender wellness-retreat** — Aman / Six Senses spa pages, sage palettes, sound-bath language.
- **Pink-script med spa** — Restylane / Allergan stock photography, cursive over botox needles, "Mommy Makeover" copy.
- **Clinical white-lab-coat** — dermatology practice sites, blue-and-white medical iconography, before/afters as the hero.
- **Vegas-luxe** — Wynn / Bellagio gold-and-marble, gilded everything, casino-floor opulence.
- **Minimalist-startup** — a Linear/Notion shell painted gold, sans-serif everything, app-style nav.

## Design Principles

1. **Earn the gold.** Gold is a commitment, not a decoration. One gold gesture per surface; never gold-on-gold-on-gold. Restraint is what makes it read as wealth instead of tackiness.
2. **Medical rigor without the lab coat.** Authority comes from precision, specificity, and calm — protocol names, time blocks, a doctor's signature. Never sterile-clinical cues (white surfaces, stethoscope iconography, "FDA-approved" badges).
3. **Discretion over exhibition.** A traveler stopping in for a half-day retreat does not want to be sold to; they want to be received. No urgency tactics, no "Book Now!" stickiness, no testimonial carousels yelling 5 stars.
4. **Editorial, not promotional.** Long-form rhythm, generous space, real photography of the actual rooms. No card grid of stock-photo services. The page reads like *Cereal* or *Robb Report*, not Groupon.
5. **Concierge tempo — feeling, then offering, then proof.** First establish the world (hero, identity), then the journeys (what is offered), then the trust (testimonials, location, FAQ). Price arrives once the visitor is already deciding, never as the lede.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Sensible defaults: focus rings on every interactive element, semantic landmarks, alt text on every meaningful image, reduced-motion respect on the flash-left hero animation and parallax cards. Contrast verified for dark gold (#C9A96E and darker) on cream (#FAF6EE-class) backgrounds — the brand palette is the highest-risk pairing on the site and must be tested at body-text and interactive-control sizes.
